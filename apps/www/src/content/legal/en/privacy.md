## 1. Overview

Mdit is built with a privacy-first, local-first approach. This policy explains how information is handled when you use the Mdit desktop app and website.

## 2. Information we collect and process

We do not require account registration to use Mdit. By default, your note content is intended to stay on your device, and we do not operate a hosted note-storage service for your notes.

When you activate, validate, or deactivate a paid license, we process the license key and activation identifier. We also send limited client metadata used by the licensing flow, including platform and user agent.

If you submit feedback from the app, we process the feedback message and any optional email address or screenshot you provide.

If you choose to send an in-app error report, we process report details such as timestamp, app version, platform, user agent, error message, stack trace, and component stack.

The desktop app may contact the update delivery endpoint (GitHub Releases) to check for and download app updates.

We do not use analytics or telemetry in the app to track your writing behavior. Infrastructure providers for website operations may process limited technical logs needed for security and delivery.

## 3. How we use information

We use limited processed information to operate, secure, and troubleshoot Mdit, to verify paid-license access, to process feedback and error reports you submit, and to support update delivery.

## 4. When data may leave your device

By default, your note content remains local on your device.

Data may leave your device when you use features that require external services, such as license activation or validation, feedback or error reporting, AI provider integrations, or Git sync.

When you use AI features with a provider you select, prompts and related context may be sent to that provider under that provider's terms and privacy policy.

When you enable Git sync, note files and commit history may be pushed to the remote repository you configure.

## 5. Sharing of information

We do not sell personal information. We do not share note content from a hosted Mdit note server because we do not operate one.

Depending on the features you use, information may be processed by: our licensing provider (Polar), the operator of the configured feedback or error-report endpoint, GitHub Releases for update delivery, and third-party AI or sync providers you choose to connect.

## 6. Data retention and your control

Because note data is stored locally by default, you control retention by managing files on your device and your own backups. If you remove local files, that data is no longer available in Mdit unless you restore it from your backup.

In the desktop app, the license key is stored in your system keyring, and the activation identifier is stored in local app storage.

For information processed by external services (such as licensing, updates, feedback, or AI/sync providers), retention is limited to what is needed for operational, support, security, and legal purposes and is also subject to each provider's policies.

You can request access or deletion for data we control by contacting [contact@mdit.app](mailto:contact@mdit.app).

## 7. Third-party services

Mdit may link to or interoperate with third-party services. Their privacy practices, terms, and data handling are governed by their own policies.

## 8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes are effective when published on this page.

## 9. Contact

For privacy questions or privacy requests (including access or deletion requests), contact us at [contact@mdit.app](mailto:contact@mdit.app) or open an issue on [GitHub](https://github.com/hjinco/mdit/issues).
